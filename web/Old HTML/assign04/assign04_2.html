Explanation

Follows here a table explaining the purpose of each skill set generally available to all characters. Even in normal D&D it can be a bit vague as to what each is really useful for. Since this is not normal D&D but instead a Star Wars variation, I believe this further documentation will prove helpful.

Skills

Appraise Ability to judge the quality, integrity, or authenticity of merchandise and sometimes other things.
Astrogate Ability to navigate safely through space and understand star charts.
Balance Ability to maintain sense of balance.
Bluff Ability to lie.
Climb Ability to climb.
Computer Use Ability to understand computers, hack into security panels or systems, etc.
Craft Ability to make a family of objects, like armor, various types of weapons, stimpacks/medpacks, explosives. Usually used to create things and then sell them.
Demolitions Ability to use, place, understand, and disarm explosives properly.
Diplomacy Ability to negotiate agreements, rearrange prices, diffuse hostile situations, etc.
Disable Device Ability to disable/destroy mechanical devices or locks.
Disguise Ability to use clothing, makeup, voice, and mannerisms to act like and pass off as someone else or simply hide one’s own identity.
Entertain Ability to use a skill like singing, playing an instrument, acting, etc. Usually used to perform for a crowd and make money.
Escape Artist Ability to get out of handcuffs, tight spaces, escape from someone trying to pin you down or hold you in place, etc.
Forgery Ability to create fake items or documents that will pass some level of inspection.
Gamble Ability to guess probabilities and win money at gambling games.
Gather Information Ability to collect information about desired topics, either directly or by hearsay.
Handle Animal Ability to understand animals and get them to cooperate.
Hide Ability to conceal oneself, either behind or under actual cover or in a crowd.
Intimidate Ability to use fear to influence another into retreating, surrendering, or conceding to perform some action.
Jump Ability to jump.
Knowledge Level of knowledge in a given field like biology, engineering, The Force, psychology, galactic “geography”, politics, etc.
Listen Ability to catch distant or subdued sounds.
Move Silently Ability to move without making noise. Difficulty increases the faster one moves.
Pilot Ability to understand space- and aircraft and use them effectively.
Profession Level of proficiency in a given occupation, like cooking, bodyguarding, writing, farming, animal trainer etc.
Repair Ability to fix (or un-fix) mechanical objects like ships, droids, construction machinery, etc.
Ride Usually, ability to ride animals and control them, but may also apply to ability to ride small speeders or similar things.
Search Ability to find or notice things by carefully looking for them.
Sense Motive Ability to sense the intentions or goal of someone else.
Sleight of Hand Ability to perform quick hand motions like pickpocketing, throwing an object, hiding something in a sleeve or pocket, etc. while avoiding detection.
Spot Ability to quickly notice something in a very short amount of time.
Survival Ability to withstand extreme temperatures, forage for food and water, track animals, and maintain sense of direction.
Swim Ability to swim.
Treat Injury Ability to evaluate wounds and treat them (usually using medpacks).
Tumble Basically the ability to use the flipping, swinging, and rolling parts of parkour.
